Privacy Policy

Customer Service Guy - Last updated July 10, 2026

This Privacy Policy explains how Customer Service Guy handles candidate information when people use the Android app to submit and track a recruitment application dossier for customer service opportunities in Lisbon, Portugal.

Customer Service Guy does not use email for application submission. The app stores application dossiers, recruiter status updates, recruiter messages, and private CV files in secured cloud services.

Developer and contact

Customer Service Guy is operated by Algorise. For privacy questions, data requests, or deletion requests, contact: algorise@usa.com.

Information the app handles

The app may handle the following information during use:

  • The candidate's full name.
  • The candidate's email address or WhatsApp contact.
  • The selected referral language, such as German or Dutch.
  • Confirmations for English communication, work eligibility, role and process understanding, and private dossier storage consent.
  • An English CV or resume file chosen by the candidate.
  • Information contained in the candidate's CV, such as name, contact details, work history, education, and skills.
  • An account identifier and, when Google sign-in is chosen, basic Google account information such as an email address.
  • A device notification token used to deliver application and message updates when notifications are enabled.

How information is submitted

When the candidate submits the application form, the app creates a private application dossier in the configured cloud backend. The CV is stored privately with that dossier. Approved recruiter accounts can view the dossier, open the CV, update the application status, and send messages inside the app.

How information is used

Candidate information is used only for recruitment-related purposes:

  • Reviewing basic language and work-eligibility requirements.
  • Preparing or submitting a referral for customer service roles.
  • Communicating with the candidate about the referral process.
  • Supporting screening, interviews, and hiring steps.

Sharing

Candidate information may be shared with recruitment or hiring teams only when needed for referral, screening, interview, and hiring purposes. Customer Service Guy does not sell candidate data and does not share it for advertising.

Storage and retention

The current production service uses Supabase Auth, Postgres, and Storage for account access, application dossiers, and private CV files. Firebase Authentication, Cloud Firestore, and Cloud Storage may be used as a fallback for compatible deployments. Firebase Cloud Messaging supplies device notification tokens for application and message alerts. The app does not send CVs through email.

Candidate information is kept only for as long as reasonably needed for recruitment, referral, communication, legal, or record-keeping purposes. Candidates can remove their CV or delete their application from the app. They can also request access, correction, or deletion by emailing algorise@usa.com.

Security

The app uses authenticated accounts, Supabase row-level security and storage policies, or Firebase security rules to restrict access. Session credentials stored on the Android device are protected using Android Keystore encryption. Candidates can access their own dossiers. Approved recruiter accounts can access applicant dossiers, open CVs, update statuses, and send messages. Algorise limits access to people who need the information for recruitment-related purposes.

Children

Customer Service Guy is intended for job candidates and is not directed to children under 13. Children under 13 must not submit CVs or personal information through the app.

Third-party services

The app uses Supabase for authentication, database, and file storage; Google sign-in when a user chooses that option; and Firebase Cloud Messaging for push notifications. Firebase authentication, database, storage, and App Check services may be used by the fallback backend. These providers may process technical information needed to operate their services. The app does not use advertising or analytics SDKs.

Changes to this policy

This policy may be updated when the app changes or when legal, operational, or Google Play requirements change. The latest version will be published on this page.
